My journey with the game

I learned chess at the age of eight from my uncle, and ever since then I have been deeply fond of the game. Of course, I did not start as early as many grandmasters do, but that never mattered much to me. I was always happy whenever I had the chance to play.

Back then, the strongest opponent I knew was my uncle. If I remember correctly, I probably lost my first twenty games against him. Learning the rules of chess is easy, mastering the game, however, is an entirely different challenge.

In 2021, I made my first online presence by joining chess.com, one of the world's most popular online chess platforms. If I remember correctly, I started with an initial Rapid rating of 1000. As I write this, my Rapid rating is around 1920, so it has been quite a journey so far. I don't think I have reached my plateau yet..not anytime soon, at least. But regardless of my rating, the joy of playing chess is something I don't think will ever fade. :)

What this space is for

While I am always happy to discuss chess openings, strategy, or the game in general, this page is dedicated to the fascinating mathematics hidden within chess. Here you'll find topics ranging from closed knight's tours and graph-theoretic ideas to counting problems, existence proofs, and other beautiful mathematical curiosities inspired by the game.
