A Simple Polynomial Problem

May 12, 2026 · 5 min read · Promit Dutta

On 10th May 2026, this question appeared on the Indian Statistical Institute's BMath & BStat UGB entrance paper.

The question

Problem: Let $P$ be a polynomial of degree $\geq 1$ with real coefficients such that $P(x) \geq 0$ for all $x \in \mathbb{R}$. Suppose $x_0 \in \mathbb{R}$ is such that $P(x_0) = 0$. Show that there exist a positive integer $n$ and a polynomial $S$ with real coefficients such that $S(x_0) \neq 0$ and $$P(x) = (x - x_0)^{2n} S(x) \quad \text{for all } x \in \mathbb{R}$$

Solution

The first step is standard. By the division algorithm for polynomials, we can write $$P(x) = (x - x_0)^{i}\, S(x)$$ where $S$ is a polynomial with real coefficients, $S(x_0) \neq 0$, and $i \geq 1$ is the multiplicity of the root $x_0$.

We must show that $i$ is an even positive integer. For the sake of contradiction assume that $i = 2n + 1$ for some non-negative integer $n$, i.e. $i$ is odd. Then $$P(x) = (x - x_0)^{2n+1} S(x) = (x - x_0)^{2n}(x - x_0)\, S(x).$$

We examine the one-sided limits of $P(x)$ as $x \to x_0$.

Since $P(x) \geq 0$ for all $x \in \mathbb{R}$ and $(x - x_0)^{2n} \geq 0$ always, the sign of $(x - x_0)\, S(x)$ determines the sign of $P(x)$ near $x_0$.

As $x \to x_0^+$, we have $(x - x_0) > 0$. Since $P(x) \geq 0$, the factor $S(x)$ must satisfy $\lim_{x \to x_0^+}S(x) \geq 0$. By continuity of $S$, $$S(x_0) \geq 0.$$

As $x \to x_0^-$, we have $(x - x_0) < 0$. Since $P(x) \geq 0$, the factor $S(x)$ must satisfy $\lim_{x \to x_0^-}S(x) \leq 0$. By continuity of $S$, $$S(x_0) \leq 0.$$

Combining these two inequalities, $S(x_0) = 0$, which contradicts $S(x_0) \neq 0$.

Conclusion: The multiplicity $i$ of $x_0$ must be even. Writing $i = 2n$ for some positive integer $n$, we obtain $P(x) = (x - x_0)^{2n} S(x)$ with $S(x_0) \neq 0$. $\square$
