Comment Policy

How discussion on Marginalia is expected to work — and why it matters.

The spirit of this space

Marginalia is a personal mathematics blog for anyone who finds mathematical ideas genuinely interesting — students, researchers, and curious readers. The comment section exists to extend that curiosity: to ask questions the post left open, offer corrections, or share related ideas.

Most people here are thoughtful. This policy exists for the rare case that someone isn't.

Writing math in comments

Good news: you can write typeset mathematics in comments. GitHub Discussions — the platform powering the comment section — renders LaTeX natively. Use $...$ for inline math and $$...$$ for display equations, exactly as in the posts. So corrections, alternative proofs, and counterexamples can all be written properly.

What makes a good comment here

  • Questions, including basic ones — if the post left something unclear, that is worth saying.
  • Mathematical corrections with precise reasoning.
  • Alternative proofs, counterexamples, or related ideas the post missed.
  • References to papers or books that go further.
  • Respectful disagreement with clear argument behind it.

What is not welcome

  • Spam, self-promotion, or comments unrelated to the post.
  • Harassment, personal attacks, or discriminatory language.
  • Sharing private information about others without their consent.
  • Threats, unlawful content, or abuse of the discussion system.
  • Repeatedly posting the same content.

Moderation

Comments may be hidden or removed at the site owner's discretion. This is a personal blog — moderation is editorial judgment, not arbitration. Moderation decisions are final and at the site owner’s discretion

Severe violations — including threats, harassment, or illegal content — may be reported to GitHub Trust & Safety.

Comments are public

All comments are publicly visible and permanently linked to your GitHub account. They may persist in external archives — such as the Internet Archive — even after deletion from the discussion thread. Please do not post anything you would not want publicly and permanently attributed to your account.

Account requirements

Commenting requires a GitHub account. All eligibility requirements — including age restrictions, which vary by country — are governed by GitHub. Please refer to GitHub's Terms of Service for the current requirements applicable to your location. By commenting, you confirm that you meet them.

Data and privacy

Comments are stored on GitHub's servers and are subject to GitHub's Privacy Statement. This blog does not independently collect, store, or process your comment data. No compensation is owed for ideas or content shared here.

Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated at any time. The version at this URL is always current. Continued commenting after any update is taken as acceptance of the revised policy.

By commenting, you confirm that you have read and agree to this policy, that you meet GitHub's eligibility requirements for your location, and that your comment is your own original contribution or is shared with proper permission.
